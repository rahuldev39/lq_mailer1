Legal News
The Supreme Court opined that in a case where Court imposes a punishment higher than minimum relying on a irrelevant factor and no other factor as enumerated in Section 32B(a to f) (of NDPS Act) are present award of sentence higher than minimum can be interfered with.
The Supreme Court reiterated that even without registration a written document of family settlement/family arrangement can be used as corroborative evidence as explaining the arrangement made thereunder and conduct of the parties.
The Supreme Court opined that in order to apply a linking factor, a Circular, unilaterally issued by one party, cannot possibly bind the other party to the agreement without that other party’s consent.
STengku Maimun Tuan Mat was appointed as Malaysia’s first female Chief Justice belonging to the Muslim Malay ethnic group.
The Jamaican Supreme court referred to Justice Dr. Chandrachud’s opinion in the Aadhar case extensively while holding its National Identification and Registration Act as ultra vires.
